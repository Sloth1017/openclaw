## 🍷 Variables That Affect Open Bar Costs

### 1. **Drink Selection (Major Impact)**

#### Beer & Wine Only
- ✅ Lowest cost (€8–€15pp)
- ✅ Simplifies service
- ✅ Great if your crowd isn't spirits-heavy
- ❌ Limits guest options
- **Example:** 4–5 wine options, 2 beers, non-alcoholic

#### House/Standard Bar
- ✅ Full liquor setup (~€20–€25pp)
- ✅ Includes beer, wine, vodka, gin, rum, whiskey, tequila
- ✅ Basic mixers (tonic, cola, sour mix, etc.)
- ❌ Not premium brands
- **Hangar likely offers this tier**

#### Premium Bar
- ✅ Top-shelf spirits (€25–€35pp)
- ✅ Craft mixers, fresh ingredients
- ✅ Upscale brands (Ketel One, Bombay Sapphire, etc.)
- ❌ Significantly higher cost
- **Good compromise if budget allows**

#### Specialty/Signature Cocktails Only
- ✅ Can reduce costs (€12–€18pp)
- ✅ Controlled drink menu = less inventory waste
- ✅ Memorable experience, Instagram-worthy
- ❌ Less flexibility for individual preferences
- **Rising trend, worth asking about**

---

### 2. **Duration of Service (Moderate Impact)**

| Duration | Typical Cost Adjustment |
|----------|------------------------|
| **4 hours** | Baseline (€20–€25pp) |
| **3 hours** | -15% discount |
| **5 hours** | +15–20% increase |
| **6+ hours** | +25–30% increase |
| **Continuous** (6pm–2am) | +40% increase |

**Amsterdam wedding norm:** 5–6 hour receptions are standard.  
**Money-saving tip:** If cocktails are 6–7pm, then limited bar after 10pm, you can negotiate a lower rate.

---

### 3. **Service Model (Moderate Impact)**

#### Option A: Open Bar (Full Service)
- **Cost:** Highest (€20–€35pp)
- **How it works:** Guests order, bartender makes drinks, you pay per person consumed (or flat rate)
- **Best for:** Generous host, lots of drinkers
- **Risk:** Over-consumption can drive costs up

#### Option B: House Liquor Only (No Premium)
- **Cost:** Mid-range (€15–€22pp)
- **How it works:** Full bar but house brands only
- **Best for:** Budget-conscious couples
- **Trade-off:** Guests notice the difference

#### Option C: Beer, Wine & Signature Cocktails
- **Cost:** Mid-range (€15–€25pp)
- **How it works:** No hard liquor self-service; bartender makes 2–3 signature drinks
- **Best for:** Trendy vibe, controlled costs
- **Risk:** Some guests may want their usual vodka soda

#### Option D: Consumption-Based (Per Drink Charged)
- **Cost:** Varies (€2–€6 per drink average)
- **How it works:** Guests order, you pay per drink consumed
- **Best for:** Small receptions or uncertain guest count
- **Risk:** Hidden costs; tab can balloon

#### Option E: Limited/Timed Open Bar
- **Cost:** €12–€20pp (lower)
- **How it works:** Open bar for cocktail hour only, then beer/wine after
- **Best for:** Budget control without stinginess
- **Common in:** Amsterdam (popular hybrid model)

---

### 4. **Guest Count (Indirect Impact)**

- **50 guests:** Higher per-person cost (less bulk discount)
- **100 guests:** Sweet spot for negotiations
- **150+ guests:** Better per-person rates (volume discount)

**Why?** Venues charge less per head for larger groups (bartender staffing scales better).

**Hangar scenario (100 guests):**  
- Per-person rate: €20–€28
- Total bar cost: €2,000–€2,800

---

### 5. **Bar Staffing Costs (Hidden Factor)**

Rarely itemized separately, but impacts final price:

| Staffing Model | Impact |
|----------------|--------|
| **In-house staff** (venue provides) | Usually included in per-person rate |
| **External bartenders** | Often adds €150–€300 per bartender for 4–5 hours |
| **1 bartender per 50–75 guests** | Standard ratio (so 100 guests = 1–2 bartenders) |

**Hangar:** Likely includes bartending in their package. **Always ask.**

---

### 6. **Day of Week & Season (Moderate Impact)**

| Timing | Cost Adjustment |
|--------|-----------------|
| **Weekday** | -10 to -20% discount |
| **Saturday** | Standard (full price) |
| **Sunday** | -5 to -15% discount |
| **September (High Season)** | Full price or premium |
| **Off-season (Jan–Feb, Aug)** | -10 to -20% discount |

**Your date (Sept 19, Saturday):** Expect full pricing. No discount likely.

---

### 7. **House Beverages & Minimums**

Some venues bundle F&B minimums into bar costs:

**Example:** "€80pp F&B minimum includes food + drinks"
- If your meal is €45pp, bar gets €35pp allowance
- You only pay extra for bar consumption beyond that

**Ask Hangar:** Does their €80–€150pp minimum (estimated) include bar, or is bar separate?

## 🤝 Negotiation Strategy Guide

### Phase 1: Information Gathering

Before reaching out to Hangar, know what you want:

**Questions to Answer First:**
1. How many guests? (100? 120? 150?)
2. What's your total F&B budget? (€8K–€12K?)
3. Guest demographic? (Heavy drinkers or conservative?)
4. Duration? (5pm–10pm? 6pm–midnight?)
5. Do you want premium or house-level liquor?

**Why this matters:** Venues negotiate differently based on your profile. A beer-and-wine couple is easier to work with than a premium-brand crowd.

### Phase 3: Negotiation Tactics (The Playbook)

#### ✅ DO: The Bundling Play
**Goal:** Reduce bar costs by combining services

**Script:**  
> "If we bundle catering + bar + room rental together, are there package discounts available?"

**Why it works:** Venues love guaranteed spend across multiple categories.  
**Expected savings:** 5–15% on total F&B

---

#### ✅ DO: The Volume Play
**Goal:** Emphasize your guest count for bulk pricing

**Script:**  
> "We're locked in at 100+ guests. Are there per-person rate breaks for parties of 100+?"

**Why it works:** Larger groups = more predictable costs for the venue.  
**Expected savings:** 10% on house bar, less on premium

---

#### ✅ DO: The Time-Limited Play
**Goal:** Reduce hours = reduce costs

**Script:**  
> "Could we do an open bar from 6–9pm (cocktail + dinner), then beer/wine after 9pm? What's the cost difference?"

**Why it works:** Venues love predictable, limited consumption windows.  
**Expected savings:** 15–25% vs. full-night open bar

---

#### ✅ DO: The Signature Cocktail Play
**Goal:** Limit menu = control costs

**Script:**  
> "Instead of a full open bar, what if we offer 3–4 signature cocktails + beer + wine? How much cheaper?"

**Why it works:** Bartenders work faster, less inventory waste, lower spirits consumption.  
**Expected savings:** 20–30% vs. full bar

---

#### ✅ DO: The Premium Brand Play
**Goal:** Skip top-shelf, save money

**Script:**  
> "We'd like house liquor (not top-shelf). Does that bring the per-person rate down from €25 to €22?"

**Why it works:** Most guests don't taste the difference; venues appreciate the clarity.  
**Expected savings:** €2–€5pp (€200–€500 total for 100 guests)

---

#### ✅ DO: The Inclusivity Play
**Goal:** Account for non-drinkers, save on waste

**Script:**  
> "About 20% of our guests don't drink. Can we adjust the bar spend to reflect actual consumption?"

**Why it works:** Honest planning = venue confidence.  
**Expected savings:** Negotiate lower per-person rate or flat fee instead of per-head

---

#### ✅ DO: Ask for Included Add-Ons
**Goal:** Get extras without paying more

**Script:**  
> "If we lock in the premium bar package, can you include [prosecco for toasts / late-night snacks / coffee liqueurs]?"

**Why it works:** Small add-ons cost venues less than you'd pay separately.  
**Expected value:** €500–€1,000 in benefits

---

#### ❌ DON'T: Lowball Aggressively
**Bad script:**  
> "Your €25pp bar is too expensive. We'll pay €15pp or go elsewhere."

**Why it fails:** Venues have fixed costs (staff, licenses, suppliers). Being disrespectful kills negotiations.

**Better version:**  
> "€25pp is above our budget. Can we explore limited bar options to fit our €18pp target?"

---

#### ❌ DON'T: Ask for Premium Bar Prices at House Bar
**Bad approach:** Demanding Ketel One prices for well vodka.

**Better approach:** Know which brands are in the house package. Accept them.

---

#### ❌ DON'T: Negotiate Alone
**Bad:** Email the venue once, hope they respond favorably.

**Better:** Request a meeting. In-person negotiations are 3x more successful.

---

### Phase 4: Contract Review (Key Clauses)

When you get the contract, look for:

#### Clause 1: "Per-Person Minimums"
**Look for:**  
- Is the €80–€150pp minimum food + bar, or separate?
- Does unused bar minimum roll forward or disappear?

**Negotiate:**  
> "If our final headcount is 95 guests instead of 100, does the minimum adjust?"

---

#### Clause 2: "Alcohol Selection & Upgrades"
**Look for:**  
- Can you provide your own wine? (Usually no.)
- What brands are included?
- Upgrade costs clear?

**Negotiate:**  
> "Can we pre-select house brands to avoid surprise upgrades on the day?"

---

#### Clause 3: "Service Charges & Gratuities"
**Look for:**  
- Is a service charge (15–20%) automatic?
- Is gratuity included or separate?
- Can you negotiate gratuity if you love the service?

**Standard in Amsterdam:** 15–18% service charge is common and non-negotiable.

---

#### Clause 4: "Leftover Beverages"
**Look for:**  
- Can you take leftover bottles home?
- Are you charged for full inventory regardless of consumption?

**Negotiate:**  
> "Can we agree on actual consumption vs. full inventory cost?"

---

#### Clause 5: "Bar Timing Flexibility"
**Look for:**  
- Can you end the bar early if consumption is low?
- Late-night premium bar pricing?

**Negotiate:**  
> "If everyone's left by 11pm, can the bar close early?"

## 💡 Cost-Saving Ideas (No Sacrifice)

### Idea 1: Pre-Dinner Cocktail Hour (Open Bar)
Then Beer/Wine Only (Dinner → Midnight)

**Breakdown:**
- Cocktail hour (6–7pm): Full bar
- Dinner + evening (7pm–12am): Beer, wine, soft drinks

**Savings:** 25–35% vs. full-night open bar

**Why it works:** Peak drinking is early; most people slow down with food.

---

### Idea 2: Signature Cocktails + Beer + Wine + Coffee Liqueurs

**Breakdown:**
- 3 house-made signature cocktails
- Full selection of beer
- 3–4 wines (red, white, rosé, sparkling)
- Digestifs: Amaretto, Baileys, Limoncello

**Savings:** 20–30% vs. full bar

**Why it works:** Guests feel special with cocktails; coffee drinks end the night naturally.

---

### Idea 3: Off-Peak + Premium Package

**Scenario:** Sunday wedding, afternoon start (2pm–7pm)

**Breakdown:**
- Daytime = beer/wine only
- Evening = premium bar for dancing crowd

**Savings:** 10–15% on day rate + better on-site management

**Why it works:** Lower daytime consumption = lower cost.

---

### Idea 4: Negotiate Consumption-Based Instead of Per-Person

**Scenario:** If your venue will agree...

**Model:**
- €15–€18pp baseline (covers 2–3 drinks per guest)
- Pay for additional drinks consumed
- Cap at agreed amount

**Savings:** Potentially 15–25% if your guests drink less

**Risk:** Could go over if guests overindulge.

---

### Idea 5: Limit Premium Spirits

**Breakdown:**
- House spirits (vodka, gin, rum, whiskey)
- Skip expensive tequila, scotch, bourbon
- One premium option (e.g., Grey Goose vodka)

**Savings:** €3–€5pp

**Why it works:** Most guests drink well brands in mixers anyway.
